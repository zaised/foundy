Alignments

Alignments are a quick and easy way to summarise your character's moral axioms and priorities. Not every character of the same alignment has to get along, and characters of opposite alignments can be firm allies- but they might have some strong disagreements on certain issues!

Lawful Good (LG):
You work to make the world better through maintaining societal order and personal honor.
Neutral Good (NG):
You always strive to do the right thing and to help others.
Chaotic Good (CG):
You value compassion, flexibility, and spontaneity.
Lawful Neutral (LN):
You uphold structure and established systems as a personal or societal ideal.
Neutral (N):
You strive to avoid preconceptions, assessing each action and issue on its own merits.
Chaotic Neutral (CN):
You view creativity and flexibility as essential tools to seize life’s opportunities.

Deities

Any character can worship a deity, though the choice is most important for @JournalEntry[gngRTq56oFDwqWJk]{Clerics}. As a cleric, the deity you choose gives you specific benefits and will affect the alignment you’ll choose later. Enter your choice of deity onto your character sheet.

The following deities are good choices to start with. If you'd like to learn more about any of them, click on their name for more information.

@Compendium[pf2e.deities.JgqH3BhuEuA4Zyqs]{Desna} is the butterfly goddess of dreams, luck, and travel. As her cleric, you swear to aid fellow travelers, explore new places, express yourself through art and song, and find what life has to offer. You also promise never to cause fear or despair or engage in bigoted behavior.

Desna's clerics must be neutral good, chaotic good, or chaotic neutral.

Also known as the Dawnflower, @Compendium[pf2e.deities.BNycwu3I21dTh4D9]{Sarenrae} is the goddess of healing and the sun. As her cleric, you swear to protect allies, provide aid to the sick and wounded, and help repentant creatures seek redemption. You promise never to create undead, lie, or fail to strike down evil.

Sarenrae's clerics must be lawful good, neutral good, or chaotic good.

@Compendium[pf2e.deities.sZrdzh0ANQnZGXJb]{Torag} is the dwarven god of crafting and defense. As his cleric, you swear to be honorable and forthright, keep your word, respect the forge, and serve your people. You promise never to tell lies, cheat someone, intentionally create inferior works, or show mercy to the enemies of your people.

Torag's clerics must be lawful good or lawful neutral.
